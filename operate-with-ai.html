02The cold read

Eight strangers read the old site first.

No context, no charity. A persona panel scored what a stranger could establish in one pass, and where the judges agreed, the gap became a requirement.

The panel

Two hiring managers, a recruiter, a skeptical engineer, an eval practitioner, a design critic, a story editor, a completeness critic. Eight cold reads of the previous site, none of them told what to like.

What they scored

Identity: who she is, in one pass. Story: what happened, and what she decided. Cleanliness: whether the pages hold together. Their consensus gaps, not taste, set the brief for this build.

03The competition

Three homepages went in. One came out.

Design direction was settled by competition, not debate. Three agents each built a full homepage, and a three-lens judge panel read them cold.

04The build

A foundation agent, then a fleet.

One agent built the shared system: the type, the tokens, the components, and the budgets every page must obey. Then a fleet built the pages in parallel, one agent per page, this page included.

The crews

Briefs came from the cold read and the census. The crews ran on Claude Code; the direction, the budgets, and the final verdicts stayed with her.

The gates

Every page faced three judges before merge: redaction, honesty, voice. A page that failed a gate went back with notes. No pass, no ship.

05The method

Direction, judgment, evidence. The typing is delegated.

Every claim is gated before it ships. It is the same shape as the day job: define what good looks like, build it, prove it.

Feedback, two sizes

Minor meant tighten this and keep going. Major meant stop, re-plan, and show the new plan before the next line. Naming which one you are giving is most of the craft of directing agents.

What I did not build

A live model on this site. A site agent that bluffs would falsify every case it hosts, so the box on the home page answers from curated replies, refuses the rest, and says so in its own header.
