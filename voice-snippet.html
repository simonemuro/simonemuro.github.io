No. 04 · Voice Snippet

One chance to be heard.

A generated answer on a live phone call is the hardest version of the GenAI problem. No screen, no scrolling back, one shot at being understood. I prototyped the concept in June 2023; the production version has been answering callers since December 2024.

SurfacePhone support, the 1-800 line
RoleOriginated the concept, co-designed the ship
Measured+3.9 pp containment lift in production
LiveSince December 2024

A caller can't scroll past a bad answer.

Phone calls were roughly 30% of support contact volume, a $70M+ opportunity sitting on a 36% baseline containment rate. And the channel ran on scripts: a caller with a specific problem heard a generic article read at them, repeated themselves, or got transferred with nothing traveling along.

Chat was the easy place to put GenAI first. A clumsy answer in chat is just a line you scroll past. On the phone, every generated word spends the caller's time and patience, and there's no taking it back. That's exactly what made the channel worth it.

Prototyped in 2023, shipped when the platform could carry it.

The concept started in June 2023 as an answer summary: instead of reading help content at the caller, generate a short, voice-tuned answer to their actual problem. That same season, my LLM savability classifier cleared Responsible AI review and shipped as a controlled experiment.

This one couldn't follow it, and the difference was risk profile. The classifier's output was machine-read JSON no customer ever saw. This put generated words directly in a customer's ear, and customer-facing generation needed more than an approval: it needed the GenAI platform path, which didn't exist yet. When the platform could carry it, it shipped, in December 2024. The concept was mine. The ship was a team's.

A screen can show detail. Voice has to earn every second.Simone, the design rule for the channel

The contract is the design.

My pieces of the production design were the behavior and the contract. The experience runs on a six-field JSON output, parsed downstream for routing and never read by a human. Every word a caller hears is a field with a job.

customer_questionWhat the caller actually asked, normalized.
action_typeDecides what the caller hears next: answer · disambiguate · handoff · off_topic
disambiguationOne spoken question to narrow a vague intent. Just one, because the caller is on the phone.
responseThe spoken answer. Two sentences by design: the caller hears it once.
task_complexityRoutes between answering and handing the call to an expert, with context.
off_topicCaught before generation, so a risky request never becomes a spoken answer.

The structure is the safety case and the content design at once. An out-of-scope request gets caught before a word is generated. Complexity routes the call to an expert with the context carried along, so the caller never starts over. And the answer stays short on purpose: one clear next step a caller can hold in their head, not an article performed at them.

It's been on the line since December 2024.

+3.9 percentage points of containment lift, measured in production. On a channel this size, small percentages are large dollars against a 36% baseline.

The quieter outcome outlived the ship. The rubric I authored to decide whether a generated answer was good enough to say out loud went on to seed the team's broader automated-evaluation rubric. Defining good for one experience became how the team defined good for all of them.
