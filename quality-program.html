No. 03 · The Quality Program

The answer sounded right.

Agents on Intuit Intelligence were taking actions in product and financial workflows across QuickBooks. Sounding right isn't a quality bar for someone's books.

RoleMethodology, tooling, program lead
When2025 – 2026
InstrumentsGolden datasets · LLM judges · simulators · blind audits
OutcomeQuality became a release gate

A judge model can grade whether an answer reads well. It's far weaker at knowing whether the agent took the right action in a payroll run or a billing workflow, and it drifts when the product underneath it changes. As the platform scaled, one judge grading answers stopped being enough.

So I built the quality discipline as a loop, not a verdict. Golden datasets carry the correct answer and the expected actions. Offline judging gets checked against human labels on production samples. Multi-turn simulators, a GenAI bot playing the customer, surface the failures that only appear three turns deep. Regression runs make sure last month's quality is still this month's.

Trust the judge only after you test the judge.

LLM judges are cheap, fast, and necessary. Before scaling them, I validated them against a 200-task human-labeled set, with the agreement bar fixed before the test rather than after.

The judges cleared it. On that evidence I cut the planned 2,000-row vendor dataset: the judges were well tuned, and the spend wasn't needed. An evaluation program should tell you where quality is and where your money isn't required.

Here's a judge run against one golden row. One gate is supposed to fail.

Three weeks to one.

Authoring golden datasets by hand was the slowest step in the loop. A designer opened a blank row and wrote everything: the scenario, the ground truth, the expected actions, the gating criteria.

So I built a pre-population pipeline. An LLM pre-fills the evaluation data first; designers review and correct instead of authoring from blank. Turnaround dropped from about three weeks to about one across 3,000+ rows, without adding headcount.

The pipeline shipped as a pull request through the same review process as platform code: +1,590 lines, reviewed and merged. A reviewer pushed on an auto-promote edge case; the fix made the pipeline safer and landed within four hours.

"This is platform code shipped through the same review pipeline as senior engineering work." from my notes · May 2026

Two weeks' notice.

In March 2026 the program's day-to-day owner went on sudden leave with two weeks' notice, while quality was the org's top priority. I sat in every handoff session, took over the pipeline, and ran the program without dropping a release.

I also tightened the foundation while holding it. Designers re-audited expert ground truth as blind KEEP or DELETE calls, original labels hidden so nobody could anchor on the expert's answer: 1,200+ rows in the first wave. If the ground truth is wrong, everything downstream of it is confidently wrong.

Shipping started requiring evidence.

By April 2026, quality scores had risen between 8 and 13 points across three skill domains.

The structural change runs deeper than the lift: a skill didn't ramp until its golden-set results cleared the launch bar. Quality stopped being a report someone wrote and became a gate releases pass through.
