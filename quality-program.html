No. 03 · The Quality Program

The answer sounded right.

A fluent, confident answer invented a date about a customer's payroll taxes. I built the eval system that catches that before it ships, and turned quality into a gate releases have to pass.

My role: built the eval system on an agentic platform's quality team. I owned the golden datasets, the LLM-judge validation, the pre-fill pipeline, and the blind audits.
ProblemFluent answers, confidently wrong
GoalMake quality a release gate
My scopeGolden data · judge validation · blind audits
When2025 – 2026
OutcomeQuality became a release gate

How I built the bar · three moves

1   I tested the judge before I trusted it.

2   I made the slowest step a review, not a rewrite.

3   I made the audit blind.

The whole thing is a loop, not a verdict

The record

+8–13 ptsquality scores across three skills, once the gate held
200human-labeled tasks that validated the judges
2,000rows of vendor labeling the validation made unnecessary
~4 hrsto delete a ground-truth-overwrite bug caught in review

What I owned

Mine, end to end

  • The golden datasets and their ground truth
  • Validating the LLM judges against human labels
  • The pre-fill pipeline, shipped as reviewed platform code
  • The blind re-audit of the ground truth

What I'd do next

  • Push the gate earlier into the design cycle, not just before launch
  • Sample the blind audit continuously instead of in waves

A year spent defining what good looks like is why I now build to that bar myself.
