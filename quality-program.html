No. 02 · Scaling the answer key behind AI evaluation

To grade thousands of conversations, you need an answer key.

An AI support agent doesn't just answer, it acts, so to ship a new version safely you grade the whole journey, what it says and what it does, across thousands of conversations. On a large fintech's AI quality-evaluation team, I owned the offline datasets and the ground truth, and authored the generator that drafts them, so evaluation could keep pace with the agents.

RoleAI quality-eval team: owned offline datasets + ground truth; authored the pre-fill generator
Built withGolden datasets · pre-fill generation · LLM-as-judge
When2025–2026
OutcomeGolden-dataset turnaround ~3 weeks → ~1 week, across 3,000+ rows

The bottleneck: you can't ship what you can't grade.

An AI support agent talks to small-business owners about their books, and it can act on their accounts. The record of the right answer, what it should say and what it should do, is the golden dataset: the answer key.

People were authoring that answer key by hand, one row at a time. It took about three weeks per dataset, and it couldn't keep pace with the agents and skills stacking up behind it.

What I built: a generator that drafts the answer key.

I wrote a generator that pre-fills the ground truth, both what the agent should say and which actions it may take, against criteria the team defined. A person validates the draft instead of writing it from scratch. That one change is the difference between authoring and reviewing.

Why the answer key has to cover what the agent does, not just what it says.

A good answer key earns its keep on the failures a quick read would miss. Two very different ones, both caught only because the ground truth records what the agent should say and do.

On the AI quality-evaluation team, I owned the offline datasets and the ground truth, authored the generator and the LLM-as-judge simulator instructions, and partnered on the quality rubrics. The eval infrastructure, the judge, and the release gate were the wider team's. A quality program this size is never one person's work.

The generator was mine, and it's the reason evaluation could keep pace with the agents.
