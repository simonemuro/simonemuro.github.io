Case No. 03  ·  Ground truth  ·  Intuit Intelligence · quality team

The answer sounded right.

A fluent, confident answer invented a date about a customer's payroll taxes. At the scale of a whole platform, the only way to catch that is a checked answer to measure against. I owned that ground truth: the answer key every skill's quality was scored on. I created it, expanded what it measured, kept it honest, and built the system that let it scale across every skill.

Problem

Fluent answers that are confidently wrong, and still read as right.

My scope

The offline ground truth: the answer key every skill's quality was measured on.

What I did

Created it, expanded what quality measured, kept it current, and trained designers to the standard.

Outcome

Quality rose +8 to 13 points across three skills, measured on the ground truth I owned.

The catch

02
What I owned

The answer key every skill was scored on.

Ground truth is the answer key: for each scenario, the answer a skill should give and the actions it should take. I owned it. I created the answers, verified them with the experts, and kept them from going stale as the platform grew. I expanded what “quality” even measured, from accuracy, completeness, and relevance out to personalization, the right tool calls, and the right actions. And I trained the designers who authored against it, so the standard held as the team scaled.

03
How it scaled

I made the slow step a review, not a rewrite.

As the platform grew, authoring the answer key by hand stopped scaling. Experts spent their time filling fields instead of making judgment calls. So I built the system that flips it: the answers get pre-filled to spec, and the expert's job becomes review and correct, not start from a blank row. Their judgment goes where it belongs, to how each answer should be graded.

Before · blank-page authoring
  • ×Open an empty row

After · pre-fill, then validate
  • ✓The answers arrive pre-filled to spec

  • ✓The same answer key feeds new-skill work

I didn't get the rules right in one pass. I refined them again and again, testing each version on a sample before it touched the real set.

04
Keeping it honest

An answer key is only as good as its upkeep.

I re-checked the ground truth in waves, a fresh keep-or-cut call on each row with the original label hidden, so no one anchored on the first answer. I built the guardrail that keeps a validated answer from ever being overwritten by a model's draft: a reviewer caught the risk, I closed the path, and added a check that fails the run if it is ever tried. And because the judges that scored every skill ran against my ground truth, I gave the eval team the human labels that helped them validate those judges before they were trusted.

✓flags surface · anything uncertain is raised for a human to resolve
