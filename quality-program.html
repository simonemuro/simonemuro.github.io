01
The gate

No skill shipped without clearing it.

Quality was the gate. A skill went live, got blocked, or rolled back based on offline evaluations, and those evals ran against ground truth: the exact answer each skill should give and the actions it should take. A wrong tax answer or a hallucinated balance costs real money and trust, so the program measured answers against that checked ground truth, not on how fluent they sounded. I owned the ground truth end to end. I created the answers, validated them with the experts, and kept them current as the platform grew. I also expanded what quality measured, from accuracy, completeness, and relevance out to personalization, the right tool calls, and the right actions, and I trained the designers who authored to the standard.

02
The build

Hand-authoring couldn't scale. So I built the system.

As the platform grew to dozens of skills, authoring ground truth by hand fell behind. Experts spent their hours filling fields instead of making judgment calls, and every new skill started from a blank sheet. I built the approach that let offline evals run at scale, in three parts: pre-fill generators that draft each answer to spec, human-in-the-loop validation where experts review and correct instead of writing from scratch, and the simulator instructions I generated to drive each eval. I codified it so the same process runs for any skill, not just the one in front of me.

I refined the generators over many passes, testing each version on a sample before it touched the real set. That iteration is what turned a one-off script into something the whole program could rely on.

03
Keeping it honest

Scale is only worth it if the output stays trustworthy.

I re-checked the ground truth in waves, a fresh keep-or-cut call on each row with the original label hidden, so no one anchored on the first answer. I built the guardrail that keeps a validated answer from ever being overwritten by a model's draft: a reviewer caught the risk, I closed the path, and added a check that fails the run if it's ever tried. And because the judges that scored every skill ran against my ground truth, I gave the eval team the human labels that helped them validate those judges.
