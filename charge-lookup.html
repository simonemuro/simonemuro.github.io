01Background

Finding the question worth solving.

The organization had a company-wide contact-reduction goal. Billing and subscription was one of the top support drivers, and charge lookup was one of the highest-volume questions inside it.

The work was hands-on end to end: I tested the journeys, pulled transcripts, synthesized expert behavior, shaped the assistant strategy, and wrote the phased PRD.

This is the NLU-era half of the billing story. The agentic sequel, built after Intuit Intelligence replaced the Digital Assistant, is its own case.

02The problem

I walked the journey myself.

I tested the real path: chat, voice, in-product search, and Google.

The answer kept sending customers to settings, articles, or a card-charge lookup. Each surface could show what was billed. None could explain what changed.

A $228 subscription could become $288 and still show no reason. That was the problem: the page showed how much. Customers were calling to ask why.

08Learnings

What I learned, and what I keep doing.

Walk in the customer’s shoes first. Typing the real questions into chat, voice, in-product search, and Google gave me the problem faster and more honestly than any dashboard. The whole journey showed how much; nowhere showed why.

The experts’ transcripts were design material, not just validation. The best answers the assistant could give were already being spoken on the phone. The transcripts showed what questions the experts asked and what solutions worked, and I designed the responses closer to what the expert provided.

Phase the product around the API reality. API onboarding is lengthy, so the PRD shipped the minimum required capabilities immediately and let each phase absorb the data as it landed. The MVP moved the numbers while the harder work was still onboarding.

And know when to stop investing in a platform. When the successor agent platform made the old platform’s ending visible, the right move was not to fight for buy-in. We banked the gains we’d already produced and built on the successor.
