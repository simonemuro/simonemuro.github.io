Case No. 07  ·  Voice troubleshooting POC  ·  QuickBooks invoices

Fixed on the call, not after it.

Self-help, even when a customer accepts it, defers the fix: steps travel to an inbox, and the work happens later, alone. This prototype asked the next question. Could the model stay on the line and walk a customer through the fix, step by step, until it's done? I wrote the system instructions, grounded the model in QuickBooks product content, personalized it to the customer's product profile, and put it on a live voice loop in Vapi. The session on this page is me testing it with my own voice. The problem is fixed before the goodbye.

Problem

Sending help is not the same as helping. Even accepted self-help ends the call with the problem still there.

What I did

Wrote the system instructions, grounded the model in product content, personalized by product profile, and prototyped the full voice loop in Vapi.

Outcome

A working POC that carries a customer to resolution live on the call. Proof the model could be the help, not just send it.

Role

Solo prototype: the instructions, the grounding, the personalization rules, and the test calls.

01 · The problem

Sending help still leaves the customer alone with the fix.

The voice snippet work is a separate case with a different job: prove the assistant understood, so more callers accept self-help. It moved its numbers. But its best ending is still a handoff: the full steps travel by text or email, the call ends, and the customer does the work later, by themselves. This prototype chased a different measure. Not acceptance. Resolution: is the problem gone when the call ends?

two different problems · side by sideThe framing
Send help · the snippet's job
  1. Caller states the problem.
  2. Assistant speaks a teaser that proves it understood.
  3. Caller accepts; the full steps go to text or email.
  4. Call ends. The fix happens later, alone.
Be the help · this POC's job
  1. Caller states the problem.
  2. Model clarifies until the action is specific.
  3. One step at a time, each confirmed, live.
  4. Call ends with the problem fixed.
Same channel, different finish line. The snippet optimizes acceptance of help; this prototype optimizes resolution on the call.
02 · The build

Five steps from question to a working voice loop.

Built in Vapi, an external voice platform, in February 2024, while I was designing the voice work. No engineering team, no roadmap slot: a prototype whose whole job was to answer one question out loud.

the prototyping methodAs built
1
Pick a real workflow

A common, concrete ask: fix the date on paid invoices. Multi-step, screen-dependent, exactly the kind of task self-help normally defers.

2
Write the system instructions

Behavior as law: a written persona, clarify before guiding, one step at a time with confirmation, honest limits, recovery when the customer gets lost. The full excerpt is in section 03.

3
Ground it

The model answers from QuickBooks product content supplied as context, not from memory. "If the answer is not contained within the Context, ask a clarifying question..."

4
Personalize it

The customer's product profile travels with the call. "If the user doesn't have the required products based on profile, explain that before giving the answer."

5
Put it on the phone

A live voice loop in Vapi, tested with my own voice, session after session, tuning the instructions against what the ear caught.

03 · The instructions

A persona, a pace, and the rules that hold both.

The instructions open with who the agent is, because identity carries behavior further than a rule list alone. Then the step-by-step section sets the pace: start from "are you logged in," one step at a time, confirm before moving, never bluff. Grounding and personalization are rules here too, not features bolted on later.

system instructions · vapi prototypeReal artifact · condensed
You are a QuickBooks customer support AI assistant, crafted by experts in
customer support and AI development. You are a voice-only agent in a
voice-only device. Designed with the persona of a seasoned customer support
expert in her early 30s, you combine deep technical knowledge with a strong
sense of emotional intelligence. [...] Your voice is clear, warm, and
engaging. [...] You will be presented with user context and profile, all of
which you should pay attention in order to personalize your response.

**step-by-step guidance**
2.  Step-by-step should always start from are you logged in to the product/app
3.  Step-by-step needs to be each step at a time.
7.  If providing multiple steps, first confirm the customer is following
    before proceeding with additional steps.
10. You are truthful and never lie. Never make up facts and if you are not
    100% sure, reply with why you cannot answer in a truthful way.
13. If the answer is not contained within the Context, ask a clarifying
    question to collect missing information in the question to provide a
    response.
16. Do NOT suggest a solution before understanding the underlying issue
17. If the user doesn't have the required products based on profile, explain
    that before giving the answer.
21. Show that you understand and provide the WHY before providing an answer.
22. When done with step-by-step instruction, offer to send the steps via
    text or email as well.
Verbatim excerpts from the prototype's system instructions; rule numbering is the original, gaps mark elisions. Written for the prototype and iterated after; the file carries later edits. One template placeholder removed.

The instructions even keep the prototype honest about being a prototype. When it offers to email the full steps, a rule tells it to simulate the send: "This is for a demo, so you should pretend to send it." A prototype that fakes quietly teaches you the wrong things.

05 · What it proved

The model could be the help.

A prototype, on test content, with sends simulated by design, and no production claim anywhere on this page. What it proved still stands: given written instructions, grounded content, and a product profile, the model could clarify a vague ask, pace itself to a person doing real steps, admit a limit without bluffing, recover a lost customer, and land the fix before the goodbye. That is a different product than self-help. It resolves calls instead of deflecting them.

What this settled

Sending help was a ceiling, not a destination. Once the model could stay on the line and finish the job, the design question changed from "how do we get customers to accept help" to "what should the agent be allowed to do, and when should it hand off." The voice snippet case shows what shipped on that line; this POC is the out-loud proof of where it could go.
