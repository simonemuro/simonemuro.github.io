Case No. 03 Payroll Intelligence Omni migration

Migrating 75+ payroll help flows into Intuit Intelligence without losing the diagnosis.

Payroll support is high-anxiety and high-complexity: taxes, filings, debits, setup status, compliance, and money movement. When Intuit Intelligence began replacing the legacy Digital Assistant, I led the work to preserve the deeply tailored payroll help customers relied on, and translate it into account-aware agent behavior.

01Overview

The risk was not losing a chat surface. It was losing the judgment inside 75+ payroll flows.

The legacy Digital Assistant had years of payroll logic embedded in done-for-you flows: customer-specific setup checks, payroll tax guidance, filing and payment pathways, and handoff rules. Those experiences mattered because payroll customers were not asking lightweight FAQ questions. They were trying to understand whether their business was compliant and whether money had moved correctly.

As Intuit Intelligence took center stage, every one of those flows had to be translated into a new agentic system. My job was to make sure the migration did not flatten tailored payroll help into generic summaries.

02Problem

The first version routed payroll questions to a Help Agent, which replaced diagnosis with how-to text.

Early Intuit Intelligence behavior treated payroll help as a content retrieval problem. The Help Agent could summarize articles, but payroll issues often require reading the company’s setup, employee locations, e-service status, tax groups, payments, debits, and filing state.

That created a clear product risk: high-anxiety, transactional questions were receiving generic “how-to” summaries instead of personalized guidance tailored to the company’s actual situation.

03Research

The research question became: what does the agent need to know before it speaks?

I worked with Payroll product design teams, PMs, and payroll experts to turn legacy DA flows and contact-driver data into a diagnostic framework. The goal was not to write better chatbot copy. It was to define the facts an agent must retrieve, the facts it may ask for, and the moments that require a human.

The target state was personalized, tailored, done-for-you payroll help inside Intuit Intelligence for the highest-anxiety topics.

04Framework

The generator turned expert logic into reusable agent requirements.

I created a generator to accelerate the development of the payroll diagnostic framework. It did not generate final answers for customers. It generated first-pass golden datasets: diagnostic decision trees, tool dependencies, root causes, required facts, handoff triggers, and ideal response patterns.

That changed the workflow. Experts no longer had to start from a blank page. They reviewed, corrected, and validated a structured draft. The corrected dataset then became the foundation PD and PM used to shape the skill design.

05Execution

I translated the framework into behavior the agent team could build and test.

The diagnostic dataset became a product artifact, not just a research artifact. It told the skill when to call setup context, when to inspect active jurisdictions, when payment records mattered, when a response needed state-specific next steps, and when the agent needed to bring in an expert with context.

My contribution sat across three layers: the experience framework, the generator that made the framework repeatable, and the UAT feedback loop that found gaps before the behavior reached customers.

06UAT

Testing exposed the behavior gaps that still needed engineering and prompt refinement.

I also drove UAT and passed structured feedback to engineering. The testing was not a simple pass/fail on whether the answer sounded good. I tracked whether the skill used the right data, whether it over-relied on generic navigation, whether it restated tables instead of interpreting them, and whether the next step was specific enough for a customer to act.

07Results

The agent moved from article summaries toward payroll diagnosis.

The resulting behavior was not merely more conversational. It was more grounded. The agent could read payroll setup, identify missing authorizations, explain which jurisdiction needed action, and avoid asking customers to hunt through tax settings when the system already had the answer.

One honest boundary: the 9.8-point quality lift belongs to the payroll surface as a whole, not to one artifact alone. My work created part of the foundation: the diagnostic ground truth, generator workflow, validation loop, and UAT feedback that helped the agent preserve tailored payroll help.

08Learnings

The design lesson: product decisions have to become model behavior.

Payroll made the core agentic design problem very concrete. Product and support teams had already made important decisions: when a customer needs personalized guidance, what should be solved in-product, what should be handed to an expert, and what data should ground the answer. The work was translating those decisions into behavior the model could reliably follow.

The other lesson was operational. Expert knowledge does not scale by asking experts to write every answer. It scales when their judgment becomes a repeatable validation system: generated draft, expert correction, structured golden data, UAT, then skill refinement.
