The receipt

Disambiguation is repair design: the behavior for the moment the system lacks the information to proceed safely. The login and password reset flow got this repair in 2024. Self-help acceptance rose from 32 to 56 percent, and containment, the share of calls resolved without an expert, from 20 to 32. Realized savings: $135,160. The same pattern had already proved out on payroll tax calls in 2022: containment 12.5 to 18.3 percent, acceptance 23 to 32.

02The line

Say it once, at talking speed.

Callers kept refusing the help the line offered: a link, sight unseen, from a bot that did not sound sure of their problem. In 2023 I wrote and pitched three GenAI use cases for the 1-800 line to change that. The voice snippet put a generated answer in the caller’s ear, among the first on the phone channel; I co-drove the build with a partner team. Hearing why a customer cancels became its own case, No. 04.

The behavior

Answer the real question first, in a short snippet shaped for voice: a teaser of the full fix, so accepting help no longer means trusting a link sight unseen. Ask one clarifying question when the intent is only half there. Hand off cleanly the moment the call needs a person: an agent request or a pressed digit transfers on the spot.

The boundary

The full answer is never read aloud. Steps that work on a screen are noise in the ear, so the phone carries the core and the rest travels by text or email. And it launched small on purpose: one low-risk intent, a throttled rollout, containment as the success metric.

03The artifact

The rules that shape a spoken answer.

A voice answer cannot be scrolled or skimmed. This is the part of the shipped prompt that shapes an answer for the ear instead of the eye.

The triage

Bare nouns never get an answer. A caller who says only “billing” or “invoices” gets one clarifying question, and no apology padding the turn.

The shape

No lists, no URLs, no numbered steps: sequence rides on spoken connectives, first, then, next. The core fits in one breath; the detail goes where a screen can hold it.

04The era

None of this was an agent.

What it was

The login and password redesign is scripted dialog: every branch written by hand, no model in the loop. The snippet is a model on rails: it wrote the words inside a call flow where fixed rules made every routing decision. Nothing on this page planned, chose a tool, or touched an account.

Why it is here

Because the questions were already the questions. Answer in one breath. Ask when the intent is half there. Repair before you route. Hand off cleanly. The same judgment, before the model could reason.
