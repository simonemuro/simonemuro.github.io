01The decision

What do you do with half an understanding?

The system knows it is login trouble. It does not know which kind. Guessing is fast and often wrong; asking spends a turn on a channel where every second is spoken. The system could read out the closest fix, route on the best guess, check the account before speaking, or send every lockout to a person. Her call: repair. 02The line

Say it once, at talking speed.

Callers kept refusing the help the line offered: a link, sight unseen, from a bot that did not sound sure of their problem. In 2023 I wrote and pitched three GenAI use cases for the 1-800 line to change that. The voice snippet put a generated answer in the caller’s ear, among the first on the phone channel; I co-drove the build with a partner team. Hearing why a customer cancels became its own case, No. 05.

The behavior

Answer the real question first, in a short snippet shaped for voice: a teaser of the full fix, so accepting help no longer means trusting a link sight unseen. “How do I add a logo to my invoices?” gets the core in one breath, upload it once from the invoice settings and it lands on every invoice after that, then the offer of the full steps by text or email. Hand off cleanly the moment the call needs a person: an agent request or a pressed digit transfers on the spot.

The boundary

The full answer is never read aloud. Steps that work on a screen are noise in the ear: no lists, no URLs, no numbered steps, sequence riding on spoken connectives, first, then, next. Bare nouns never get an answer either: a caller who says only “billing” gets one clarifying question, no apology padding the turn. And it launched small on purpose: one low-risk intent, a throttled rollout, containment as the success metric.

What it was

Scripted dialog, with one generative step on rails. The login and password redesign has every branch written by hand, no model in the loop; the snippet wrote its words inside a call flow where fixed rules made every routing decision. Nothing on this page planned, chose a tool, or touched an account.

Why it is here

Because the questions were already the questions. Answer in one breath. Ask when the intent is half there. Repair before you route. Hand off cleanly. The same judgment, before the model could reason.

pattern 01repair · disambiguation

One shaped question, then action.

Four problems share the opening sentence. Listen for the shape of the question: three options, one breath, then action on the answer. No apology, no open prompt.

transcript
  1. caller

    Hi, I am locked out of my account, and I have a client waiting.

  2. agent

    I can help with that. Which sounds closest: you forgot your password, your reset email never arrived, or the account says it is locked?

  3. caller

    The second one. I asked for a reset email twice, and nothing came.

  4. agent

    Thank you. Reset emails sometimes land in spam, so check there first for a message from our team. If it is not there, I can send a fresh link to the mobile number ending in four two one seven. Shall I send it?

  5. caller

    Yes, send it.

  6. agent

    Done. The link is on its way, and it stays active for thirty minutes.

pattern 02error recovery · a mishear

Own the mishear, keep the call.

The recognizer hears bega for vega. Listen for who owns the error: the agent does, re-confirms letter by letter, and moves on. The caller is never told they said it wrong.

  3. caller

pattern 03escalation · with context

Hand off the context, not just the call.

The agent reaches its boundary and says so. Listen for the spoken summary that travels with the transfer: the person picks up already knowing, and the caller never repeats.

transcript
  1. caller

    The reset link finally worked, but when I sign in, it still says my account is locked.

  2. agent

    Thank you for trying that. A lock that remains after a successful reset needs a specialist, so I am bringing one in now. Here is what I am passing along: your reset went through, the new password is good, and the account still shows a lock. You will not need to repeat any of that. One moment.

  3. expert

    Hi, this is Dana. I can see the reset went through and the lock is still active, so I am clearing it on my side right now. Give it about ten seconds, then try again.

  4. caller

    That did it. I am in. Thank you.

04The flow

The whole flow, one screen.

The login and password reset flow that produced the numbers up top, drawn as its states. Every fallback lands somewhere; nothing lands at a dead end.

artifact · the snippet prompt, the rules that shape a spoken answer · redacted excerpt 
Your role is to provide a short, accurate response using a friendly and
conversational tone.

If the question is a standalone product name or object with no clear action
("billing", "invoices", "help with my account"), tell the customer you are
happy to help but need more details. Do not apologize. Do not answer until
you clearly understand the action to be taken.

Once the action is clear:
1. Be brief and conversational. Provide an answer that is a teaser snippet
   of the full answer: very concise, only the most important information.
2. Do not use bulleted or numbered lists in your response.
3. Do not provide any URLs in the response.
4. Do not list step-by-step instructions. Use first, then, next, finally...
   instead of numbers.
5. After providing a teaser snippet, offer to send the more detailed
   solution through text or email.

Keep words simple and specific. Use active verbs, and avoid jargon,
gerunds, and passive sentence constructions.

The stack

Telephony in, an ASR transcript, an NLU intent with a confidence score, a scripted dialog policy making every routing decision, and TTS reading the answer back. The 2023 snippet added one generative step inside this same loop, on rails. Designing the behavior meant writing for every stage: what the recognizer can miss, what the gate can trust, what the voice can carry.

The dials

Acceptance: the share of offered help that callers take. Containment: the share of calls that end with no expert. Handle time is the third dial; this work was scored on the first two, and the factbox numbers are those two moving.
