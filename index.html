What I build

The four artifacts every serious AI team needs

01

Prompts & agent instructions

System guidance, tool-use conditions, structured output, refusal boundaries, and tone rules that make model behavior explicit enough to test.

02

Golden datasets & ground truth

Source-of-truth answers, diagnostic decision trees, and judge-checkable gating criteria that give a team a shared definition of good.

03

Evaluation & quality loops

Rubrics, LLM-as-judge, human labeling, multi-turn simulators, and failure taxonomies that turn response quality into a release gate.

04

Working prototypes

Skills, tools, and demos built in Claude Code, Cursor, Vapi, and Voiceflow, so teams test the experience before they commit the quarter.

About

I connect the technical with the human.

I translate complex AI systems into experiences that genuinely help the person on the other side of the screen. Nine years of it: Meta, Apple (Siri), Sam's Club, and Intuit across QuickBooks and Credit Karma, growing from hands-on conversation designer to leading the team and the discipline. What the AI says, when it acts, where it stops (handing off, refusing, or asking first), and how we prove it's good enough to ship.

My bias is action. When LLMs arrived, I prototyped GenAI classification a year before our platform approved it; the concepts shipped when the platform caught up. When agentic systems arrived, I learned the repo, wrote the YAML, built the tool, and shipped a working skill in five days. Strategy first, always: map the problem to what the technology can genuinely do, then prove it with a build.

I have led that work end to end and led the people doing it: designers and design technologists I coached daily, a promotion championed through, hiring loops run, and a craft standard the team still builds to.
