Three generations of AI, and I worked through all three

Across all three, I made the same call: start with the problem, not the technology.

I'm a design technologist: I map the customer's problem to what the technology can genuinely do, then build it. The tools changed three times across nine years; I changed with them, and the principle never did.

2016 – 2022 · ML & NLU

Intent by intent

I started in machine learning: training language models at Meta to flag off-policy content, and speech recognition and synthesis at Apple. Then NLU and chatbots at Sam's Club and Intuit, where the problem was holding a customer through a scripted flow and catching them when they stepped off it. Intent models were brittle, the flows deterministic, the fallbacks hard to handle.

META · APPLE · SAM'S CLUB · INTUIT
2023 – 2024 · LLM

Prompts replace training

When LLMs arrived, I moved from training models to writing prompts; they handled the nuance and the off-script turns. The new problem was reliability, consistency, and safety: feeding data securely, keeping hallucinations in check. First GenAI classification in the cancellation flow, first GenAI voice on the 1-800 line, regulated prompt systems at Credit Karma.

INTUIT · CREDIT KARMA
2024 – 2026 · AGENTIC

Behavior becomes a system

When autonomy raised the stakes, I made behavior something you can trace and measure: the flow has to be accurate, relevant, clear, and safe every time. The work became data and quality: golden datasets, validated judges, quality as a release gate. Then I built the proof myself: an agent skill, built solo, with its own eval. Now I direct AI agents to build, and this portfolio is the proof.
