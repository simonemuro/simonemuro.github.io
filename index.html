About

I'm a staff content designer and model UX writer who thinks in systems. Language isn't the frosting on an AI product — it's part of the system. My job is to design the behavior of AI support experiences for billing, voice, subscriber lifecycle, and account-sensitive workflows: the words customers hear, the patterns and personas behind them, the training data that teaches the model what "good" means, the rubrics that measure it, and the decision logic that routes when AI should hand off to a human.

Different companies, one job: closing the gap between what an AI product can do and what it actually says to a person. I trained language models at Meta, taught Siri to speak at Apple, owned retail support assistants at Sam's Club, engineered shipped consumer LLM experiences at Credit Karma, and at Intuit grew from conversation designer through senior, staff, and conversational AI lead — now leading AI response-quality evaluation for QuickBooks' agentic assistant, managing a team of designers, and serving as PM-for-IVA across classification, contact-driver, and agentic-skill work.

How I Work

Six principles I design AI conversations against — the backbone of a design blueprint I authored so a team could review and validate AI responses against a shared bar, not by taste.

01

Cooperative by default

Every response has to move the customer's actual goal forward — not just be technically correct.

02

Contextual continuity

The system remembers the journey: the session, the account, and the moment the customer is in.

03

Asymmetric expertise

The customer is the expert on their intent; the system is the expert on what it can actually do.

04

Progressive disclosure

Start with the minimum useful answer; expand only when the customer signals they want more.

05

Repair-ready

Misunderstanding is expected, not exceptional. Every path has a graceful way to recover.

06

Emotionally attuned

Tone adapts to the moment — urgency, confusion, frustration, relief — without losing clarity.
