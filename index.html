About

I've spent the last nine years making AI understandable and useful to the people on the other side of the screen. I grew from a hands-on conversation designer into a leader who shapes how AI behaves for products used by millions: what it says, when it acts, when it hands off to a person, and how a team proves it's good enough to ship.

My strength is range. I work where customer needs, model behavior, and business outcomes meet, and I move easily between them: framing the problem, designing the experience, engineering the LLM prompts, building AI-native prototypes, and running the evaluation that holds it all to account. I've led that work end to end, and I've led the people doing it, coaching a team while staying close enough to the craft to build alongside them.

I'm at my best turning ambiguity into something a team can build: a clear read on the problem, a standard for what good looks like, and the cross-functional trust to ship it. That instinct has held across every role and every generation of the technology, in service of one thing: AI that's genuinely helpful, honest about its limits, and good enough to stake a business on.

How I Work

I find the problem worth solving, build a clear path to it, and bring the right people together to get there. I'm happiest owning an outcome end to end, from the first research question to the metric we move after launch. My superpowers are curiosity and ownership.

01

Start with the problem, not the technology

I understand the customer's real goal before reaching for a tool. New tech is only useful once I know what the customer is actually trying to do.

02

Data and actions decide everything

An agentic system is only as good as the data it can reach and the actions it can take. So I map the customer's goal, the data, and the actions early.

03

Reliable data is non-negotiable

A model reasoning over wrong or missing data fails quietly, and at scale. I treat data quality as part of the design, not an afterthought.

04

Build fast, test honestly, iterate

I would rather ship something testable early and learn from real cases than polish in a vacuum.

05

The best work is cross-functional

Strong AI experiences come from design, product, engineering, data science, and domain experts pulling as one. I put real effort into those partnerships.

06

Curiosity and ownership

Curiosity pulled me from design into NLU tuning, prompt design, and agentic systems. Ownership is what carries a problem all the way to a shipped, measured result.
