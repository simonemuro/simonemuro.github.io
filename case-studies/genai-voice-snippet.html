Voice AI · Conversation Design · Model UX

Shipping GenAI voice support with concise, measurable answers

I co-designed one of Intuit’s first GenAI voice support experiences, defining disambiguation, voice-friendly generated summaries, structured model output, handoff behavior, and response-quality criteria for a high-volume support channel.

Role

Conversation designer, model UX writer, prompt designer, evaluation-rubric designer.

Scale

Inbound voice support represented about 30% of contact volume, with a 36% baseline containment rate.

Outcome

Reached Bayesian significance with a +3.9 percentage-point containment lift on tested intents.

Context

Traditional voice assistant flows were constrained by rigid NLU paths and scripted responses. Customers with specific support questions often heard generic answers, repeated themselves, or transferred without enough context for the next support step.

GenAI created a real opportunity, but voice raised the stakes. A generated answer that looks acceptable on a screen can feel long, evasive, or risky when a customer has to listen to it in real time.

Design constraint Voice has to earn every second.

Approach

I designed the experience around structured behavior: clarify when needed, generate only what belongs in a spoken answer, and preserve context for routing and evaluation.

01

Clarify before generating

Use disambiguation when the caller’s question is vague or the system cannot safely infer intent.

02

Generate spoken summaries

Favor brief, action-oriented guidance over long support-article readouts.

03

Structure the output

Capture fields for question, action type, disambiguation, response, complexity, and off-topic detection.

04

Escalate with context

Hand off when the system cannot confidently help, while preserving what the customer already said.

Key decisions

Make brevity a quality requirement

The response needed to be short enough to hear and remember. That changed the prompt strategy and the evaluation bar.

Separate answer quality from routing quality

A generated answer could be useful, but the system also needed to know whether to continue, clarify, or transfer.

Design for unsupported questions

Off-topic detection and handoff criteria reduced the risk of the model producing confident but unhelpful voice responses.
