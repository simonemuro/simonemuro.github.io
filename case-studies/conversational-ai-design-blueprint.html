Content Systems · Conversation Architecture · AI Standards

Scaling conversational AI quality through reusable standards

I created a conversational AI design blueprint to help teams move from one-off flow decisions to a shared system for how AI should clarify, answer, recover, hand off, and be evaluated.

Role

Author, craft lead, model UX writer, conversation design systems thinker.

Scope

Voice and tone, response patterns, disambiguation, repair, handoff, evaluation, and validation.

Value

A reusable standard for teams designing across NLU assistants, GenAI responses, and agentic systems.

Context

As the team moved from intent-based assistants to GenAI and agentic systems, craft knowledge was scattered: what makes a response helpful, when to clarify, how to recover, when to hand off, and how to evaluate quality.

The team needed more than voice-and-tone guidance. It needed an operating model for AI behavior that could travel across products, channels, and generations of technology.

System idea Turn tacit craft judgment into shared product behavior.

The blueprint

The blueprint connected content craft to model behavior, so teams could review AI experiences through the same practical questions.

01

Clarify

When intent is ambiguous, ask the smallest useful question instead of guessing.

02

Ground

Answer from verified product, policy, account, or support context rather than plausible language.

03

Act

Know when the user needs an action, tool, workflow, or handoff instead of more explanation.

04

Evaluate

Define quality through rubrics, examples, failure patterns, and launch-readiness checks.

Key decisions

Frame language as system behavior

The blueprint treated content as part of the product logic: what the AI knows, asks, says, does, refuses, and escalates.

Make standards usable in review

I organized patterns so designers and partners could use them in critique, launch review, and model evaluation, not only as a reference deck.

Bridge old and new AI systems

The guidance translated NLU-era craft into the GenAI and agentic era: repair, routing, escalation, and quality still matter, but the control surfaces changed.

Review questions

Clarity Will the customer know what happened, what it means, and what to do next?
Grounding Can the system verify the facts it is presenting?
Recovery What happens when intent, data, or confidence is missing?
Evaluation What examples and rubrics define whether this behavior is good enough?

Result

The blueprint gave the team a shared way to design, review, and validate conversational AI quality as the work moved from scripted flows into prompt and agent behavior.
